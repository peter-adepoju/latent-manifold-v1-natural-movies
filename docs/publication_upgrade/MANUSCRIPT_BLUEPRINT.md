# Manuscript blueprint

## Working title

Layer-specific manifold geometry of mouse V1 population responses during natural movie viewing

## Abstract skeleton

Natural vision drives cortical populations with complex spatiotemporal structure, but how cortical population geometry reflects naturalistic visual statistics remains unclear. We analysed Allen Brain Observatory V1 calcium-imaging responses during repeated natural movie viewing. We constructed session-level trial tensors, extracted real image-derived features from the presented movie frames, quantified neural manifold geometry, and evaluated stimulus encoding/decoding under strict repeat, movie-block, and session-level controls. We compared classical and contrastive latent representations, tested geometry-event enrichment against circular-shift nulls, and aligned neural manifolds with analytic and artificial vision model representations. The resulting framework identifies which aspects of V1 manifold structure are reproducible across sessions and which are explained by low-level visual statistics, temporal continuity, or layer-specific population coding.

## Core caveat

Do not claim causal stimulus control from correlation or decoding alone. Frame the geometry-stimulus analyses as descriptive unless perturbational or causal evidence is added.
